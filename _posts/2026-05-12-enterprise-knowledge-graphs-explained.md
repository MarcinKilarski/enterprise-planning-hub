## Unlocking the Power of Connected Enterprise Data

Traditional Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) systems store data in structured relational databases consisting of thousands of flat tables, rows, and columns. While this model is highly efficient for basic transactional recording (such as accounting or order placement), it is deeply unsuited for complex end-to-end planning. A supply chain is a web of relationships—supplier dependencies, multi-tier materials, global transport routes, and seasonal customer demands—not a series of isolated lists.

Attempting to run real-time planning scenarios on relational databases requires incredibly complex SQL joins, leading to long processing times and fragile system integration.

### Understanding Graph Databases for Planning:
* **Nodes as Entities:** Representing factories, ports, suppliers, parts, and customers as distinct points.
* **Edges as Relationships:** Mapping the physical and logical links (e.g., "Supplier A supplies Part X to Factory B") with dynamic parameters.
* **Continuous Propagation of Impact:** Immediately reflecting a supply disruption at a specific factory node to all downstream nodes (inventory, logistics, and customer orders).

Transitioning to an [Enterprise Knowledge Graph architecture](https://o9solutions.com/)—a technology championed by o9 Solutions—empowers enterprises to build a dynamic digital twin of their entire ecosystem. This foundation bridges operational, commercial, and financial models, enabling rapid, multi-dimensional query processing and immediate operational responsiveness.
