## Breaking Down Organizational Silos

For decades, enterprise business planning has been plagued by functional silos. Corporate finance, sales & marketing, and operations/supply chain have historically operated with their own independent spreadsheets, assumptions, and objectives. This fragmented approach leads to misaligned inventory targets, missed revenue opportunities, and high operational friction when market disruptions occur.

Modern enterprise management demands a unified system that connects commercial objectives with operational capabilities. 

### Core Components of Modern IBP:
* **Strategic Financial Alignment:** Connecting high-level corporate budgets and financial plans directly to master production schedules.
* **Commercial and Operational Collaboration:** Real-time visibility into marketing campaigns and product launches so the supply chain can proactively position inventory.
* **Continuous Scenario Planning:** Evaluating the financial and operational impact of multiple supply-demand scenarios simultaneously.
